Inspiration

A few months ago I decided that I wanted to wake up to something less abrasive than alarm sounds and more engaging than music. I considered the radio, but didn't want to be stuck with one station or show.

So I scoured the web and the App Store looking for an app that would do one simple thing: allow me to wake up in the morning to a podcast of my choosing. I wasn't satisfied with what I found, so I figured I'd try to make it myself.

Functionality

Next Up was designed to be a clean, intuitive tool, well-suited to its particular purpose. It integrates with podcasts already managed in the native Podcasts app and plays in the native Music app.

Each alarm has options to repeat on particular days of the week, play the newest episodes first, only play uneard episodes, choose snooze duration, max volume and fade-in duration, and more. This allows it to accomodate for podcasts of all different types, whether daily, weekly, serial, stand-alone, or those dealing with news or current events.

Ads and Privacy

Next Up will never use intrusive banner or pop-up adds of any kind. There are just too many of those in the world already. Nor will it ever collect or sell anyone's personal data. That's just not a cool thing to do.

Next Up

At-a-glace view of which alarm is up next and when. Double-tap to dim the screen. Double-tap again to return to its original brightness.

Alarms

Tap the switch to disable or enable an existing alarm. Swipe left to delete an alarm. Tap the '+' to create a new one..

Alarm Settings

Tailor every alarm to its podcast. Newst first for current events, oldest first for serial or stand-alone podcasts you want to catch up on.

Podcasts

Auto-imports all the podcasts you have downloaded in the Apple Podcasts app. See the number of episodes, and how many are new.
