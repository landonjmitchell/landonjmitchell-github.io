What It Is

Next Up is a full-featured podcast alarm clock, allowing you to be woken up by any podcast you've downloaded in the Apple Podcasts app. It provides a more pleasant, more engaging, and more customizable option than alarm sounds, music, or live radio. Wake up to a meditation, the news, a comedy show, or the voice of LeVar Burton reading you a new short story.

Next up is now live in the App Store, and can be found here.

What It Does

Next Up integrates with podcasts already managed in the native Podcasts app and starts playing them in the native Music app when the alarm is triggered.

Tailor each alarm to its podcast. Schedule alarms to repeat on the days a new episode is released. Play the newest episodes first for news, sports, and current events, or start with the oldest to catch up on serial or stand-alone podcasts.

Lower the volume for louder podcasts, or keep it high for the quiet ones. Fade-in the volume gradually for a gentler wake-up, or for podcasts with especially long or loud intros.

Where It Came From

A few months ago I decided that I wanted to wake up to something less abrasive than alarm sounds and more engaging than music. I considered the radio, but didn't want to be stuck with one station or show.

So I scoured the web and the App Store looking for an app that would do one simple thing: allow me to wake up in the morning to a podcast of my choosing. I wasn't satisfied with what I found, so I figured I'd try to make it myself.

Next Up

At-a-glace view of which alarm is up next and when. Double-tap to dim the screen. Double-tap again to return to its original brightness.

Alarms

Tap the switch to disable or enable an existing alarm. Swipe left to delete an alarm. Tap the '+' to create a new one..

Alarm Settings

Tailor every alarm to its podcast. Newest first for current events, oldest first for serial or stand-alone podcasts you want to catch up on.

Podcasts

Automatically accesses all the podcasts you have downloaded in the Apple Podcasts app (with your explicit permission of course). See the number of episodes, and how many are new.

Ads and Privacy

Next Up will never use intrusive banner or pop-up adds of any kind. There are just too many of those in the world already. Nor will it ever collect or sell anyone's personal data. That's just not a cool thing to do.
